Marks

Channels

  • Color
    • Represents the political party that won the electoral votes in a given state
    • This is a good mapping choice because the majority of people easily recognize blue and red as Democratic and Republican, respectively. Using a US Map where each state is filled in with the appropriate representative color, the visualization easily conveys which political party would have received the electoral votes.

Colormap(s)

  • Categorical
    • Represents the political party (Blue or Red) that won the electoral votes in a given state
    • This is a good mapping choice because in American culture, blue and red are clearly representative of the two political parties: Democrats and Republicans. The two contrasting colors are easily distinguishable. Where votes were split in Nebraska, the fill of blue and red stripes is also a great choice that represents the situation well.

Marks

  • Points

Channels

  • Horizontal and Vertical Position (Both)
    • Horizontal- represents the exit velocity
    • Vertical- represents the launch angle
    • This is a good mapping choice because the scoring value is based on the angle of the ball's launch as well as the speed off the bat. By making the x-axis the exit velocity and the y-axis the launch angle, it creates a 2D graph on which each ball can be plotted based off of these two variables. The horizontal and vertical positioning also fits well with the data types of the variables, as it clearly shows the increasing velocity from left to right and the increasing angle from bottom to top.
  • Color
    • Represents the scoring value
    • This is a good mapping choice because the color of the point plotted on the graph shows whether or not the scoring value of that batted ball was on the higher or lower spectrum of LWTS in comparison to the average value. The two distinct colors clearly show which batted balls were better than others on the graph, which would have looked a lot messier and unclear if the scoring value was not represented by differing colors.

Colormap(s)

  • Divergent
    • Represents the spectrum of scoring values
    • This is a good mapping choice because the two extremes of the scoring values, higher than average or lower than average, are represented by purple and orange, respectively. The average, or midpoint of the scoring value is marked by a neutral white color, and the saturation of the colors increase as the value gets farther away from the average. Thus, viewers can clearly tell what range of angles and velocities produce the highest scoring batted balls on the graph, and which range of angles and velocities produce the lowest scoring batted bats.
